# Product

## Register

product

## Users

USP (University of São Paulo) procurement and contracts staff. Internal administrators who manage purchasing workflows, import documents, create contracts, and generate official procurement documents. They work in a structured institutional environment and need reliable, efficient tools.

## Product Purpose

Internal document generation system for USP procurement workflows. The application streamlines the lifecycle of university purchases: from importing demand documents and purchase orders, through managing items and suppliers, to generating final contract documents. Success means staff can complete procurement workflows accurately and efficiently without manual document formatting.

## Brand Personality

Institutional, trustworthy, professional. The system represents USP's institutional authority — it should feel reliable and formal, not trendy or playful. Voice is clear, direct, and functional. Tone is neutral-professional.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboard aesthetic (cream backgrounds, gold accents, gradient cards)
- Bootstrap admin template look (over-decorated cards, gratuitous shadows)
- Playful startup interfaces (rounded corners everywhere, bouncy animations, emoji)

## Design Principles

1. **Institutional Trust**: Every interaction reinforces that this is an official USP system. Consistent, predictable, reliable.
2. **Functional Density**: Information-dense layouts that respect the user's expertise. No wasted space, no unnecessary decoration.
3. **Consistent Vocabulary**: Same components, same patterns, same behavior across all screens. Users learn once, use everywhere.
4. **Accessible by Default**: Keyboard navigable, screen reader compatible, high contrast. No accessibility as afterthought.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA compliance as baseline
- Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements
- Screen reader support with proper ARIA labels
- High contrast ratios (USP green on white passes AA)
- Consistent focus indicators
- Responsive down to tablet (1024px minimum target)
